Hillary Rosen finally gets the fact that suing grokster, napster, kazaa, whatever music stealing app is in this week, etc is not going to make any kind of dent in the online music sharing phenomenon. People are going to continue sharing music until the labels under the auspices of the RIAA ( the almost universally loathed big Five) dramatically change their business. They can continue to play whack-a-mole suing various companies, but people aren't going to be told what kind of music they can listen to for much longer.
